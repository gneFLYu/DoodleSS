What the colors mean

Dot colors report the class’s derived HFPSS fate. They do not encode algebraic order, torsion length, or coefficient size.

DKLLW dot / fat dot / circle / square — respectively k, k[[j]], k[[j]]{j}, and W(k); shape is independent of fate color.
Multiplication lines — vertical: 2 in the same cell; slope 1: h1; slope 1/3: h2; dashed in 2BSS: hidden extension.
Green — permanent cycle, backed by an accepted permanent-cycle proposition.
Gray — fate unresolved; no accepted death or permanent-cycle proof.
Rose — supports an accepted differential and disappears on the next page.
Purple — receives an accepted differential and disappears on the next page.
Solid charcoal arrow — accepted differential claim.
Amber dashed arrow — candidate or under-review differential.
